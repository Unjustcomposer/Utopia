# Letter of Intent

**Date:** July 24, 2026
**To:** The Founders, NexusAI Inc.
**From:** Office of the Chief Supply Chain Officer, Walmart Inc.

**Subject: Non-Binding Letter of Intent for Enterprise Pilot of NexusAI Supply Chain Digital Twin**

To the NexusAI Team:

This Letter of Intent ("LOI") sets forth the preliminary understanding between Walmart Inc. ("Walmart") and NexusAI Inc. ("NexusAI") regarding the proposed deployment of the NexusAI Automated Tariff Impact & Supply Chain Twin platform within Walmart's global supply chain operations.

## 1. Pilot Scope
Walmart intends to deploy NexusAI in a 90-day paid pilot to model second-order macroeconomic shocks (specifically focusing on tariff escalations and trans-Pacific shipping delays) against live inventory levels. 
NexusAI will connect directly to a dedicated sandbox instance of Walmart's SAP S/4HANA ERP environment to read `InventoryStock` and autonomously generate buffer recommendations.

## 2. Commercial Terms
Subject to successful legal and cybersecurity review, Walmart intends to enter into a Master Services Agreement (MSA) with NexusAI.
*   **Pilot Fee:** $150,000 USD for the 90-day evaluation period.
*   **Production Deployment:** Upon successful completion of the pilot KPIs (defined below), Walmart intends to negotiate an Enterprise License Agreement estimated at $1,250,000 USD Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) for global rollout.

## 3. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
To convert the pilot to a production enterprise license, NexusAI must demonstrate:
1.  **Speed to Insight:** Ability to model a complex 30-variable macroeconomic shock across 10,000 SKUs in under 15 minutes.
2.  **Explainability:** The platform must provide exact causal attribution (via Jacobian mathematics) for every recommended inventory buffer to satisfy internal risk and compliance boards.
3.  **ERP Resilience:** The SAP OData integration must maintain 99.9% uptime without triggering SAP rate limits or gateway timeouts during peak inference loads.

## 4. Next Steps
Upon execution of this LOI, Walmart's IT Security Architecture team will commence the vendor risk assessment, after which we expect to finalize the MSA and Statement of Work (SOW) by Q4 2026.

We are highly impressed with the differentiable architecture NexusAI has built, which fundamentally outperforms the discrete-event simulators we have evaluated in the past. We look forward to a successful partnership.
